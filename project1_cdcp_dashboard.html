Clinical informatics insights
🦷
Ontario carries disproportionate load
Ontario holds 41% of all approved applicants (2.63M) yet its utilization rate (~31%) lags BC (~33%), suggesting EMR workflow and provider capacity gaps in high-density urban centres. Digital intake optimization at dental clinics could close this gap.
📊
Atlantic provinces show highest relative uptake
NS, NB, NL show utilization rates 30–33%, comparable to BC despite smaller provider networks. Suggests these populations had higher unmet need — consistent with rural oral health access literature and baseline CDCP targets.
⚠️
56% of approved members have not yet received care
With 6.4M approved and only 2.8M served, a 3.6M gap remains. Key barriers documented in JCDA research include provider capacity, preauthorization complexity, and patient-side awareness — all addressable through digital health interventions and EMR optimization.
